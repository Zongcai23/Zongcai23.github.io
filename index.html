Biomedical microrobotics, optical manipulation, and AI.

I study intelligent micro-scale robotic systems for biomedical contexts, with research interests shaped by optical-tweezers manipulation, digital-twin modelling, microscopy perception, and AI-enabled control.

Collaboration

Who I am looking for

I am looking for collaborators who can bring real biological or biomedical experimental scenarios where micro-scale manipulation may be useful.

Cell biology / biomedical labs

Groups with cell handling needs, biological samples, assays, or microscopy-based experimental workflows.

Microfluidics / lab-on-chip

Researchers developing chips, channels, sorting devices, sample-preparation platforms, or on-chip imaging systems.

Microscopy / optical systems

Collaborators interested in visual feedback, optical manipulation, imaging, or experimental validation at the microscale.

The most useful collaboration would combine a clear biological question or experimental platform with my background in optical microrobotics, digital twins, perception, and AI-enabled control.

Research

Core themes

Biomedical microrobotics

Micro-scale robotic systems for biomedical contexts, especially where precision, safety, and interaction matter.

Physics-informed perception

Simulation, microscopy image formation, pose estimation, and data-efficient sim-to-real learning.

AI and shared control

Reinforcement learning, haptic interaction, digital twins, and human-in-the-loop robotic control.
